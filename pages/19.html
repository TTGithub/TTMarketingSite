Equity Investment Firm

ThruThinkSM is designed to meet the needs of an Equity Investor. This can be a simple net asset Company investment where either a portion of the Equity or 100% of the Equity is being purchased by the Investor or a more involved full Balance Sheet purchase of a Company. There is a choice of five time horizons for the investment and an Exit Sale provision. There can be three classes of Equity ownership in a Project. Each class can have a different percentage of Equity and a different percentage of Gain on Exit Sale of the Company. The latter is designed to provide an Equity manager an incentive upon the Exit Sale of the Company. Multiple Projects can be saved and compared. The ThruThink Deal ScoreSM can be used as a comparison guage between Projects.

Sales, Cost of Goods Sold or alternatively EBITDA may be adjusted and managed along with Debt levels and repayment options and an array of Subordinated Debt options along with PIK Interest and Warrants. There is a Performance Earn Out provision for either the Seller or strategic investor.

The Individual Premium, Corporate Basic and Corporate Premium are subscriptions to use the ThruThinkSM site which will enable the User can make changes on up to 10 Projects for the Individual Premium and an unlimited* number of Projects on the Corporate Accounts.

ThruThinkSM Deal Score

The ThruThink EvaluationSM evaluates the "Deal" or comparative benefit to the Owner as presented by the User Inputs and calculates the ThruThinkSM Deal Score. It is not necessarily a score for the quality of the Company itself, but a score for the "Deal". In other words, an excellent Company can receive a poor Deal Score because of the level of the purchase price or valuation, improper debt or equity structure, capital expenditures, owner draws, etc. The ThruThinkSM Deal Score is available with the Individual Quick Deal and the ThruThinkSM subscription.

The ThruThinkSM Deal Score uses proprietary algorithms and relationships that evaluates multiple factors involving historical information, the transaction itself and the projections which result in an overall grade or score for the "Deal".

Individual Companies in Investment Portfolio

For each Company subject to investment, Data Inputs are inputted separately and saved as Individual Projects. This can be done by the company or by the Investment Manager (IM). Whoever inputs the data will become the "Author". For Corporate Accounts, the Author will give access permission to specific individuals, for example, the IM (when prepared by the company), the Sector VP and the CEO.

Level 2 — Investment Sector

The IM would create a Summary Report for his or her investments and save it such as "Rebecca's Companies". The saved Summary Report would list specific key summary Data Points, listed for each of the IM's investments and an average for each Data Point for all of the listed investments. The IM would be the \Author of this Report and give permission to others such as the Sector VP and the CEO. The IM would have access to each of the Individual Projects that provided Permission to the IM.

Level 3 — Total Firm

A Sector VP would create a Summary Report of the all the Investment Managers Summary Reports within that Sector, i.e.. a summary of multiple summaries. For this report, for each Investment Manager with investments in the Sector, the average for each of the Summary Data points by IM will be listed under the Data Point and an average of all of the IM's will be shown. The Sector VP will then save this as a Sector Report and provide Permission to the CEO. The CEO can then prepare another Summary Report showing the averages by Sector that represents the whole company and if desired can in turn give Permissions to the Report back through the management chain. The CEO and Sector Managers would have access to each Individual Project and the Investment Manager Summary Reports as long as the Permissions for each have included those individuals.
