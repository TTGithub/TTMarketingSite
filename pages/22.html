Restaurant Chain

ThruThinkSM is designed to meet the needs of a restaurant or chain owner. Each Restaurant can be set up as a different Project and with the Corporate Accounts, the Projects can be combined to represent the total Restaurant Chain. There is a choice of five time horizons for the investment and an Exit Sale provision. There can be three classes of Equity ownership in a Project. Each class can have a different percentage of Equity and a different percentage of Gain on Exit Sale of the Company. The latter is designed to provide an Operations Manager an incentive upon the Exit Sale of the Company. Multiple Projects can be saved and compared. The ThruThink Deal ScoreSM can be used as a comparison guage between Projects.

Sales, Cost of Goods Sold (Cost of Service; food, labor, etc) or alternatively EBITDA may be adjusted and managed along with Debt levels and repayment options and an array of Subordinated Debt options along with PIK Interest and Warrants. There is a Performance Earn Out provision for either the Seller or strategic investor.

The Individual Premium, Corporate Basic and Corporate Premium are subscriptions to use the ThruThinkSM site which will enable the User can make changes on up to 10 Projects for the Individual Premium and an unlimited* number of Projects on the Corporate Accounts.

ThruThinkSM Deal Score

The ThruThink EvaluationSM evaluates the "Deal" or comparative benefit to the Owner as presented by the User Inputs and calculates the ThruThinkSM Deal Score. It is not necessarily a score for the quality of the Company itself, but a score for the "Deal". In other words, an excellent Company can receive a poor Deal Score because of the level of the purchase price or valuation, improper debt or equity structure, capital expenditures, owner draws, etc. The ThruThinkSM Deal Score is available with the Individual Quick Deal and the ThruThinkSM subscription.

The ThruThinkSM Deal Score uses proprietary algorithms and relationships that evaluates multiple factors involving historical information, the transaction itself and the projections which result in an overall grade or score for the "Deal".

Individual Restaurant

For each Restaurant, Data Inputs are inputted separately and saved as an Individual Project. This can represent individual restaurants or any individual budgeted area. Whoever inputs the data will become the "Author" such as the Restaurant Manager. For Corporate Accounts, the Author will give access permission to specific individuals, for example, the Area Manager, VP of Operations and the Owner or CEO.

Level 2 — Area or Region

The Area Manager would execute a Combination Process to combine all of the individual Restaurants of a particular City or Area and save it such as "Alpha Area Restaurants". The saved Project would be a Combination Project and reflect the sum of all of the Restaurants in the Project. All input functionality is available in a Combination Project therefore Sales, Expenses, Debt, etc. can be fully adjusted, added or changed at the combination level. A Summary Report can also be created at this level. A Summary Report would list specific key summary Data Points, listed for each of the Restaurants and an average for each Data Point for all of the listed Restaurants Departments. The Area Manager would be the Author of both the Combination Project and the Summary Report and give permission to either or both to others such as the VP of Operations or the Owner or CEO. The Area Manager would have access to each of the Individual Restaurant Projects that provided Permission to the Area Manager.

Level 3 — Total Company

The VP of Operations or the Owner or CEO would execute a Combination Process to combine his or her areas and save it such as "Beta Region Restaurants". The saved Project would be a Combination Project and reflect the sum of all of the Restaurant Areas in the Project. All input functionality is available in a Combination Project therefore Sales, Expenses, Debt, etc. can be fully adjusted, added or changed at this combination level. A Summary Report can also be created at this level. A Summary Report would list specific key summary Data Points, listed for each of the Areas and an average for each Data Point for all of the listed Areas. The Senior VP will be the Author of both the Combination Project and the Summary Report and give permission to either or both to others such as the Owner or CEO. The VP of Operations, Owner or CEO would have access to each of the Individual Restaurants and Areas and Summary Reports that provided Permission to the VP of Operations, Owner or CEO.
