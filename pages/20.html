University / Classroom

ThruThinkSM is designed to illustrate the often times complex relationship between the business performance of Sales/Expenses and Income Taxes, Cash Flow, Net Income and the Balance Sheet in an understandable and clear manner. This is a perfect classroom exercise to show the way busness really functions and provides the opportunity to discuss the effect of Sales, Cost of Goods Sold, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Accounts Payable, Income Taxes, Debt repayment and Equity Positions. Each student can formulate their own business model from a set of facts provided by a case study. An increasingly advanced set of facts as the class goes along can provide countless opportunities for indepth discussion of business principles and entail synergistic learning for the students.

Level 2 — Total Class

There is a ThruThinkSM provided Instruction Manual with Lesson Plans for each Class session. Using the Lesson Plans, the Instructor will teach the basic principals for each section of the case study and conduct Student discussions with alternative examples at each section. As the Students go through the Classes, they will be making the Inputs to their Individual Projects through their own online account. The Instructor will have access to review each Students Project and intermittently grade the project, provide feedback and a Final Grade. The Instructor can make a Summary Report showing a Summary of each of the Student Projects as well as an average for all of the Students in the Class. A Summary Report would list specific key summary Data Points, listed for each of the Students Projects and an average for each Data Point for the entire Class. The Instructor would be the Author of the Summary Report and give Permissions to the Department Head. The Instructor and the Department Head would have access to each of the Individual Student Projects that provided Permission.

Level 3 — Total Department

The Department Head would create a Summary Report of the all the ThruThinkSM Classes within that Department, i.e.. a summary of multiple summaries. For this report, for each Class, the average for each of the Summary Data points by Class will be listed under the Data Point and an average of all of the Classes will be shown. The Department Head will then save this as a Department Report and provide Permission to the Instructors in order to view all of the Class Averages. The CEO can then prepare another Summary Report showing the averages by Sector that represents the whole company and if desired can in turn give Permissions to the Report back through the management chain. The CEO and Sector Managers would have access to each Individual Project and the Investment Manager Summary Reports as long as the Permissions for each have included those individuals.
